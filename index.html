Lecture Notes

Short summary of the main lecture topics:

  • Introduction to spatial heterogeneity and non-stationarity
  • Difference between stationary and non-stationary relationships
  • Why spatial relationships may vary across geographic areas
  • Global models versus local models in spatial analysis
  • Introduction to Geographically Weighted Regression (GWR)
  • How to compare GWR with OLS using AIC, R², and residual patterns
  • Use of Moran’s I to identify clustered spatial patterns
  • Application of GWR in forensic GIS
  • Case study on overdose mortality and socioeconomic variables
  • How local models support place-specific forensic interpretation
